\section*{Equilibrium Analysis: 2025 vs 2019 Designs}

\subsection*{2025 Design (Single Stable Equilibrium)}

The 2025 kite design exhibits a \textbf{single, well-behaved equilibrium point}:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Final elevation: $62\text{--}67\degree$
    \item Angle of attack: $2\text{--}4\degree$ (efficient glide mode)
    \item Data clustering: $\sim 85\%$ of filtered data converges to this point
    \item Convergence quality: Excellent ($\text{kite\_vel} < 1.5$ across all wind speeds)
\end{itemize}

This indicates a \textbf{stable, controllable system} that reliably settles into a single operating mode regardless of wind speed.

\subsection*{2019 Design (Bifurcated Equilibria at Low Wind)}

The 2019 kite design exhibits fundamentally different behavior depending on wind speed:

\subsubsection*{At $v_w = 9$ m/s (Bifurcation):}

The system exhibits \textbf{two distinct equilibrium points}:

\begin{itemize}
    \item[\textbf{Point 1:}] High angle of attack mode
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Final elevation: $66\text{--}70\degree$
        \item Angle of attack: $23\text{--}26\degree$
        \item Character: Aggressive flight mode
        \item Population: $\sim 4$ data points
    \end{itemize}
    
    \item[\textbf{Point 2:}] Low angle of attack mode
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Final elevation: $78\text{--}83\degree$
        \item Angle of attack: $11\text{--}14\degree$
        \item Character: Efficient glide mode
        \item Population: $\sim 2$ data points
    \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

This bifurcation suggests the system can settle into either configuration, leading to \textbf{unpredictable steady-state behavior}.

\subsubsection*{At $v_w = 20$ m/s (Loss of Stability):}

The 2019 design \textbf{fails to converge} at higher wind speeds:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Kite velocity: $2.1\text{--}7.4$ m/s (all significantly $> 1$, indicating poor control convergence)
    \item Elevation response: Highly scattered ($62\text{--}79\degree$)
    \item Angle of attack: Chaotic variation ($10.6\text{--}27.6\degree$)
    \item Conclusion: \textbf{Control system insufficient} for stable operation at higher wind speeds
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Summary}

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\begin{tabular}{lcc}
\hline
& \textbf{2019 Design} & \textbf{2025 Design} \\
\hline
\textbf{Low Wind ($9$ m/s)} & Bifurcated (2 modes) & Single stable point \\
\textbf{High Wind ($20$ m/s)} & Unstable & Stable \\
\textbf{Overall Stability} & Poor & Excellent \\
\textbf{Controller Adequacy} & Insufficient & Sufficient \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

The 2025 design represents a \textbf{significant improvement}, eliminating both the bifurcation problem at low wind speeds and the control instability at high wind speeds.
